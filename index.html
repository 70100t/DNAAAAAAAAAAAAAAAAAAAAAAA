Scene 5: DNA Limitations

DOCTOR:
"DNA phenotyping is a significant tool, but it comes with important limitations. Understanding these limitations helps us use the data responsibly."

üß¨ 1. Polygenic Traits

Most physical traits, such as face shape, nose, and hair type, are controlled by multiple genes. A single DNA sample cannot fully predict these traits, so the sketches are estimates.

üå± 2. Environmental Factors

Lifestyle, nutrition, age, injuries, or medical conditions can all change appearance. DNA cannot predict these, meaning the predicted traits may not exactly match actual traits.

‚öñÔ∏è 3. Accuracy & Ethics

The sketches are predictions, not actual proof. Misuse can lead to profiling, bias, or wrongful assumptions. DNA can guide the case, but should not be used as reliable proof.

üß™ 4. Degraded DNA

Sometimes hair or DNA samples are damaged, making predictions less reliable. This is why Proper preservation and precise techniques are essential.

Summary:
DNA gives investigators a prediction, but is not a proof of a crime. The sketch should be used carefully, keeping in mind their limitations to avoid false assumptions.
